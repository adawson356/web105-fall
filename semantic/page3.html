Water towers often have a tank at the top that connects to vertical pipes. Treated water enters into an aloft tank via system primary feeder pipes. Water exits the water tower via piping. Depending on how a water tower is built, there may be space leftover for stairs or even rooms below the tank.

Sometimes a tank atop a hill or mountain is already at a high enough altitude to negate the need for a base tower. In this case there is no base. Sometimes there is no tank. A resevoir atop a mountain serves the same purpose as a water tower.
